# Scaling Laws for Reward Model Overoptimization

Gao, Schulman, Hilton (2022). Key finding: as you optimize more against a reward model (proxy), the proxy score continues to increase but the gold-standard score (true preference) peaks and then decreases. The relationship follows predictable scaling laws.

This is empirical evidence for Goodhart's Law in the ML context. The paper quantifies the proxy-target divergence and shows it's a function of model size, dataset size, and optimization steps.
