# The proxy is not the target

A recurring pattern across AI research, institutional economics, and epistemology: whenever a proxy is optimized as if it were the real target, the proxy diverges from the target in proportion to the optimization pressure applied.

This is not a failure of any specific proxy — it is a structural property of proxy-target relationships. The meta-idea connects Goodhart's Law (philosophy), RLHF reward hacking (AI), and mechanism design (economics) as instances of the same underlying dynamic.

The bridging insight is that the *rate* of divergence depends on the tightness of the feedback loop and the sophistication of the optimizer. This suggests a unified framework for reasoning about proxy failure across domains.
