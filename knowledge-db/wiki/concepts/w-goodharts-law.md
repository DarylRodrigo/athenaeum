# Goodhart's Law

"When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." — Marilyn Strathern (1997), generalizing Charles Goodhart's original observation about monetary policy.

## Original formulation

Goodhart's original (1975) formulation was specific to monetary policy: any statistical regularity used as a target for policy will break down once it becomes a target. The regularity held when it was merely observed; it fails when it is intervened upon.

## Strathern's generalization

Strathern (1997) stripped the domain specificity and gave us the universal form. The insight is not about money or policy — it is about the relationship between measurement and optimization. Any system that optimizes a proxy will eventually diverge from the target.

## Implications

Goodhart's Law is not a warning to choose better metrics. It is a statement that *no* metric is immune to this dynamic under sufficient optimization pressure. The question is not "which proxy is safe?" but "how do we design systems that account for inevitable proxy-target divergence?"
